Competitive Advantages

Built-In Moat. No Direct Competitor.

✦
Only Venue of Its Kind
No establishment in Brentwood or surrounding communities combines upscale wine service, premium coffee, and live entertainment. The nearest comparable concepts are 25–40 minutes away in Walnut Creek, Concord, or the Tri-Valley.
▲
Dual-Revenue Model
The hybrid café-by-day, venue-by-night model generates consistent daily revenue while maximizing per-square-foot returns across all dayparts.
★
Affluent Underserved Market
Brentwood's median household income is 178% of the national average with 67% of households earning over $100K. The community actively seeks premium local experiences.
⍑
507 Free Parking Spaces
The Lone Tree Crossings development provides abundant free parking—a major advantage over urban entertainment venues. Most co-tenants close by evening, freeing the lot for events.
☾
Evening Activation of Daytime Plaza
C'est La Vie becomes the anchor night-time tenant, creating a symbiotic relationship with the landlord by driving foot traffic during otherwise dormant hours.
Beer & Wine Only Strategy

By excluding hard liquor, C'est La Vie simplifies licensing (ABC Type 41 is over-the-counter), reduces security risks, lowers insurance premiums, and aligns with family-friendly zoning.

Community Network

Patanisha Pierson's role as Vice Mayor and her annual Juneteenth celebration (5,000+ attendees) provides built-in community relationships most new venues spend years building.
